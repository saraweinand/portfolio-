    Hello, I'm Sara! I create beautiful & functional websites!

    I've been interested in designing the web for as long as I can remember, but it took some rad professors and a welcoming group of design students to help me get on the track to being a professional! I don’t just “make things pretty.” I research, organize, problem solve, and craft experiences. My passion is designing for web and the mapping out the interaction between user and interface. As a designer, I pay attention to detail and give every pixel the attention it needs to create great work. I'm a recent graduate of Eastern Washington University where I earned my B.A. in Visual Communication Design. During my time at EWU I put a great deal of emphasis on the design and development on the web. I've recently located to Bellingham, Washington with my husband and two dogs.

    Ideation & Sketching

    I like to put all my ideas on paper. I start with a concept and immediately define what that concept is going to achieve. What is the call to action? I’ll list all of the goals and aspects of the design and the importance of the content. This part of the process usually involves narrowing down the intended user and what it is I want them to accomplish.

    Information Architecture

    After the initial ideation phase, I organize the information by laying out a site map that includes all of the relevant information that will be present on each page.

    User Flows

    When the content is organized by page, I then begin sketching and re-sketching how the user can reach each page from different parts of the site.

    Detailed Wireframes & Redlines

    I personally like to do all my initial wireframing on paper so I can record all the extra notes that will go into the design and interaction of the page, such as transitions and content ideas. (These we cleaned up so you can read them.)

    Prototype & Mockups

    I have strong HTML/CSS skills, so my preferred prototyping technique includes creating a high fidelity mock-up by developing it. However, when dealing with client interactions, I often will create a mockup in photoshop with notes detailing how the parts will work.
