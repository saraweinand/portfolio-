    Concept & Sketching

    I always have a million ideas initally, so I like to put it all on paper. I start with a concept and immediately define what that concept is supposed to achieve. What is the call to action? I’ll list all of the goals and aspects of the design and the importance of the content. This part of the process usually involves narrowing down the intended user and what it is we want them to accomplish.

    Information Architecture

    After the initial ideation phase, I'll start to organize the information by laying out a site map that includes all of the relevant information that will be present on each page and how each page should hold the content. I typically revisit this step a few times to rearrange and refigure how it will all work together in harmony.

    User Flows & Task Analysis

    When the content is organized by page, I then begin sketching out the ideal path to the desired action. I'll continue sketching and re-sketching how the user can reach each page from different parts of the site to ensure a seamless and functional flow throughout the site. How will the user try to achieve their goal and how can I deliver the content to serve them?

    Wireframes & Redlines

    I personally like to do all my initial wireframing on paper so I can record all the extra notes that will go into the design and interaction of the page, such as transitions and content ideas. During the wireframing phase, I'll usually revisit my user flows to make sure I took into consideration all of the details and content on the page. My redlines are used to help me translate my designs into a prototype quickly and effectively.

    Prototype & Mockups

    I have strong HTML/CSS skills, so my preferred prototyping technique includes creating a high fidelity mock-up by developing it and pushing it to Github. However, when working directly with clients, I will typically create a mockup in photoshop and iron out the design details before creating the hi-fidelity prototype.

The Stremlers wanted to be set apart from their competition and allow customers to interact with their site and gain information about the company.
